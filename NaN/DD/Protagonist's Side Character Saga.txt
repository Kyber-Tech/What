### Prologue: The Betrayal and the Truck

Elliot Grayson, 27, was a barista with dreams bigger than his paycheck. He wasn't a hero—just a guy with a receding hairline, a worn-out manga collection, and a girlfriend, Vanessa, who swore she loved him. Spoiler: she didn't. He caught her locking lips with Chad, the gym-bro barista with a jawline that could cut diamonds, behind the espresso machine. "You're boring, Elliot," Vanessa said, tossing her hair like a shonen villain revealing their master plan. Chad flexed, his apron straining like it was auditioning for *Dragon Ball Z*.

Heartbroken, Elliot stormed into the rain, muttering about "deserving better." Cue **Truck-kun**, the isekai reaper, hauling artisanal coffee beans. It swerved, and Elliot's last thought was, *"Death by coffee? Poetic."* Splat. Darkness.

He woke in a void, expecting a goddess or a glowing system screen. Instead, a bored voice droned: "Welcome to Aeloria, fantasy world of magic and destiny. Name: Elrik. Role: Nobody. Skills: None. Here's some ambition. Don't waste it, useless. I hate my job as the Goddess narrator, your the one who I will be the lab-rat that i will spend my frustrations on." No stats, no cheat codes—just a burning desire to be *somebody*. Elrik clenched his fists. "I'll be the greatest hero Aeloria's ever seen!" Spoiler: he wouldn't.

### Chapter 1: The Side Character's Grand Delusion

Elrik materialized in Aeloria's bustling town of Solhaven, all cobblestone charm and adventurers flexing their plot armor. His inventory: a linen tunic, patched trousers, one copper coin, and ambition so intense it felt like a status effect. "This is my isekai glow-up," he declared, strutting to the Adventurer's Guild.

The guild hall was a shonen fever dream: spiky-haired swordsman Kael (protagonist energy radiating), tsundere elf mage Lyria (tragic backstory loading), and brooding rogue Zane (smoking a cigarette that defied medieval logic). Elrik approached the counter, chest puffed. "Sign me up for the hero track!"

The clerk, a unibrow-sporting woman named Gert, didn't look up. "Name?"  
"Elrik, future legend!"  
She handed him a wooden badge: *D-Rank: Miscellaneous*. "Try not to die," she muttered, pointing to a quest board with gems like "Clean Guild Outhouse" and "Deliver Bread to Leeroy Jenkins." Elrik's ambition flared. "Bread delivery? My tutorial arc!"

The quest warned: *"Leeroy Jenkins is… intense."* Elrik, picturing a kindly old man, grabbed a sack of baguettes and marched to a shack littered with broken swords and scorch marks. He knocked. The door exploded outward as **Leeroy Jenkins**, a wiry lunatic with a wild beard and a tattered cape, screamed, **"LEEEEROOOOY JEEEKINS!"**

"Uh, bread delivery?" Elrik stammered. Leeroy snatched a baguette, wielded it like Excalibur, and charged a nearby goblin cave, yelling, "TIME TO SLAY!" Elrik, sensing his moment, followed. "I'll back you up, Leeroy!" Goblins swarmed. Leeroy swung the baguette, somehow one-shotting three. Elrik tripped over a root, face-planting into mud. A stray fireball (probably Lyria's) sent him tumbling into a ditch.

Leeroy emerged victorious, munching the baguette. "Good bread, kid!" he shouted, tossing Elrik a copper coin before sprinting off, screaming his name again. Kael's party, fresh from slaying a dragon, passed by. "Nice assist, bread boy," Zane smirked. Elrik, muddy and defeated, clutched his ambition. "Next time.”


### Chapter 2: The Bakery Incident

Elrik pivoted. "Heroes need allies," he reasoned, targeting Solhaven's bakery, run by Mira, a cheerful girl with flour-dusted braids. She seemed like a love interest—classic isekai subplot bait. "I'm Elrik, destined for greatness," he said, biting into a melon bread.  
Mira blinked. "Cool. Can you sweep the floor?"

Elrik swept like it was his training montage, imagining a future where he and Mira cheered Kael's party from the sidelines. He overheard them planning a quest to the Cursed Forest for the Crystal of Eternity. "I'll tag along, save the day, and steal Mira's heart!" he told his broom.

The forest was a nightmare of sentient vines and glowing eyes. Kael's sword glowed like it had a sponsorship deal. Lyria's fireballs rewrote physics. Zane's new leather jacket defied budget cuts. Elrik, wielding his broom, shouted, "I'm here to help!" A vine snagged him, dangling him upside down. He swung the broom, yelling, "Take that!" The vine dropped him into a mud pit.

Kael's party grabbed the crystal, saved the world, and didn't notice Elrik crawling out, broom broken. Back at the bakery, Mira handed him another melon bread. "You're… muddy."  
"Hero stuff," Elrik mumbled. His ambition whispered, *"Keep going."*


### Chapter 3: The Festival of Futility

Solhaven's Festival of Heroes was Elrik's big break. "This is my montage," he declared, ignoring his D-Rank badge. The festival had tournaments, magic contests, and a "Hero's Speech" event. Elrik signed up for the speech, scripting a banger about his destined greatness. He practiced in his attic, shouting, "I am Elrik, the one who will surpass the gods!" His landlady, Mrs. Grubble, banged the ceiling. "Quiet, loser!"

At the festival, Kael won the sword tournament with one swing. Lyria's firework spell lit the sky. Zane won "Best Pose" by existing. Elrik's speech was last, after a bard's lute solo. He stepped onto the stage, heart pounding. "People of Aeloria, I—"

**"LEEEEROOOOY JEEEKINS!"** A dragon crashed the festival, and Leeroy sprinted in, wielding a *rye loaf*, screaming, "I'VE GOT THIS!" The crowd cheered. Kael's party leapt into action, swords and spells blazing. Elrik kept shouting his speech, but a fireball singed his tunic, and he dove behind a fruit stall. Leeroy, somehow, clubbed a dragon wing with his loaf, stealing the spotlight. Kael finished the beast, naturally.

The festival resumed. Elrik's speech slot was replaced by a dog show. Mira found him sulking. "Heard Leeroy interrupted you," she said, offering a cookie. "Fireworks?" Elrik, cookie in hand, nodded. His ambition flickered. *"Next year."*

### Chapter 4: The Side Quest Spiral

Elrik doubled down. "Side quests build character," he told himself, taking every D-Rank job: delivering letters (wrong town, sparked a feud), herding chickens (one pecked his eye), unclogging sewers (rat stole his coin). Each failure chipped at his ambition, but he pressed on.

He overheard Kael's party planning to storm the Demon Lord's castle. "This is it," Elrik thought. "I'll sneak in, save the day, and rewrite my story." Armed with a rusty dagger from a ditch, he followed. The castle was gothic chaos—spikes, fog, ominous organ music. Kael's sword now had a theme song. Lyria's spells bent reality. Zane's smirk was a war crime.

Elrik hid in a barrel, waiting for the final battle. The Demon Lord monologued about eternal darkness. Kael countered with hope. Lyria cried about her mentor. Zane posed. Elrik leapt out, dagger raised. "I'll end this!" The Demon Lord flicked him into a wall. Kael's party won, saved the world, and left Elrik concussed in rubble.

Mira bandaged him at the bakery. "You're persistent," she said. "Stupid, but persistent." Elrik's ambition, battered, whispered, *"One more try."*


### Chapter 5: The Rival NPC

Elrik met his nemesis: Tobin, an NPC so irrelevant he made Elrik look like a main character. Tobin's job? Holding signs for merchants. His catchphrase? "Eh, whatever." Tobin's beige cardigan was *beiger* than Elrik's, his dialogue so generic it hurt. "The weather's nice," Tobin droned, holding a sign for "Grok's Discount Potions."

Elrik, enraged by Tobin's effortless irrelevance, vowed to out-NPC him. He took a sign-holding gig, standing opposite Tobin. "I'll hold this sign so hard, I'll be legendary!" A wind gust blew his sign into a river. Tobin's sign stayed upright. "Eh, whatever," Tobin said, sipping tea.

Kael's party passed by, planning to fight a Sky Fortress. Elrik, desperate, followed, sign in hand. The fortress was a floating nightmare of lasers and golems. Elrik waved his sign ("Buy One Sword, Get One Free!") to inspire the party. A golem crushed it. Kael's team won, naturally. Tobin, somehow there, held a sign reading "Victory Sale!" and got a nod from Zane. Elrik sulked. "How is *he* better at this?"

Mira, handing him a coffee, said, "Maybe stop competing with Tobin. He's… nothing." Elrik's ambition twitched. *"I can be less than nothing."*

### Chapter 6: Leeroy's Revenge

Leeroy Jenkins struck again. Elrik took a quest to deliver "the Legendary Sourdough of Power" to Leeroy, hoping to piggyback on his chaos. Leeroy's shack was now a shrine to broken bread and goblin skulls. "LEEEEROOOOY JEEEKINS!" he screamed, snatching the sourdough and charging a troll den.

Elrik followed, yelling, "I'm your sidekick!" Leeroy, wielding the sourdough like a warhammer, obliterated trolls. Elrik tripped into a pit of troll snot. Leeroy emerged, bread intact, shouting, "GOOD LOAF, KID!" and tossed Elrik a crumb. Kael's party, coincidentally slaying a nearby hydra, didn't notice.

Back at the bakery, Mira laughed. "You and Leeroy are a disaster." Elrik, snot-covered, muttered, "He's stealing my destiny." His ambition, now a flickering ember, refused to die.

### Chapter 7: The World Tree Fiasco

Aeloria's World Tree was dying, and Kael's party was tasked with reviving it. Elrik, assigned to "water nearby shrubs," saw his chance. "I'll water the World Tree itself!" he declared, lugging a bucket. The tree was a glowing monolith, guarded by dryads. Kael's sword sang. Lyria's spells sparkled. Zane… posed.

Elrik, splashing his shrub, shouted, "I'm helping!" A dryad mistook him for a threat and yeeted him into a pond. The party revived the tree, saved the world, and left. A butterfly landed on Elrik's nose as he floated in the pond. "You again," he muttered, too tired to care.

Mira, drying his tunic later, said, "You're not cut out for this hero stuff." Elrik stared at his bucket. "Maybe you're right.”

### Chapter 8: The NPC Epiphany

Months of failure piled up. Elrik's ambition was a ghost. He'd been sidelined in every major event: Sky Fortress (carried supplies), Vampire Queen (swept battlefield), Meteor Crisis (made coffee for the party). Kael's face was on Aeloria's coins. Elrik's? On a "Lost Dog" poster, mistakenly.

At the bakery, Mira asked, "Why keep trying?" Elrik stared into his coffee. "I was supposed to be *someone*." Mira shrugged. "You're the guy who buys stale bread. That's something."

It clicked. Aeloria's heart wasn't its heroes—it was the NPCs. The bakers, blacksmiths, even Tobin with his stupid signs. Elrik's ambition shifted. "I don't need to be a hero. I just need… this." He swept the bakery, chatted with Mira, watered his houseplant. When Kael's party fought a multiversal threat, Elrik watched from the bakery, eating a cookie. A butterfly landed nearby. He smiled. "Not my problem."

### Epilogue: The Side Character's Peace

Years later, Elrik was Solhaven's unsung fixture. No statues, no legends—just a guy who swept Mira's bakery and shared her coffee. Leeroy Jenkins, now a town legend, charged past one day, screaming, "LEEEEROOOOY JEEEKINS!" with a baguette. Elrik laughed. "Go get 'em, old man."

Kael's party, now gods, visited. "Nice shop, bread guy," Kael said, tossing a gold coin. Lyria nodded. Zane smirked. They left for another world-ending quest. Elrik handed the coin to Mira. "Stale bread fund." As the sun set, he sat outside, butterfly on his knee, content in his beige-cardigan life. He'd found peace—not as a hero, but as Elrik, the guy who was *there*.

**The End. (Or is it?)**
