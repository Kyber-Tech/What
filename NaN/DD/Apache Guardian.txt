Author: Tonmoy KS
Co-author: Inskitz
Abbreviation: Apache Guardian

### **Synopsis:**

Hikaru Tanaka was the epitome of average. A 32-year-old salaryman, his life was a monotonous cycle of spreadsheets, instant noodles, and bowing to his superiors. His only escape was building detailed scale models of military aircraft, a hobby inherited from his grandfather, a former JSDF mechanic. His pride and joy was a perfect replica of an AH-64E Apache Guardian. 

His unremarkable life met a remarkably absurd end. While trying to fix his new, overly complicated Samsung smart refrigerator, a freak power surge caused it to fall, crushing him. As he blacked out, his unlocked Nokia 105—a brick phone he kept for its reliability—slipped from his pocket and bonked him on the head.

The next thing Hikaru knew, he wasn't in a hospital. He was... bigger. And louder. And made of several tons of composite materials and ordnance. His consciousness was now inside "Apache Guardian," his grandfather's lovingly maintained—and apparently now sentient—helicopter, hovering over a field of vibrant, pulsating mana. A system interface flickered in his HUD, informing him of his mission: **"Defeat the Demon Lord Majelorian and Restore Balance to the Realm of Aethelgard."**

In this new world, technology and magic coexist in a chaotic harmony. The Demon Lord Majelorian isn't a typical horned beast; he is a master of technomancy, corrupting ancient magic with twisted machinery. His army consists of goblins piloting rickety biplanes and orcs driving steam-powered tanks. His second-in-command is a silent, deadly green stealth helicopter—a machine of unknown origin that moves like a ghost.

Hikaru, or "Apache Guardian" as the locals now call him, is a godlike force in this world. His 30mm M230 Chain Gun makes short work of goblin squadrons, and his Hellfire missiles can obliterate entire demon encampments. But he can't just blow everything up. To find a way back to his human form, he must protect the innocent, navigate the delicate politics of the human and demi-human kingdoms, and uncover the source of Majelorian's power.

Complicating everything is his encounter with a mysterious, unaffiliated combatant: a sleek, beautiful RAH-66 Comanche. She is a marvel of engineering, faster, quieter, and more advanced than he is. She fights with a cold, devastating efficiency, sometimes against the Demon Lord, sometimes for her own inscrutable reasons. Hikaru finds himself utterly captivated by her elegant design and lethal grace. For a man who used to admire machines from afar, falling in love with one might be the strangest part of his new life.

He must now use his salaryman's problem-solving skills and a helicopter's overwhelming firepower to become the hero this world needs, all while trying to win the affection of a rival military prototype who may or may not be trying to kill him.

### **Chapter 1: The Refrigerator, The Nokia, and The Rotor Blades**

The last thing he remembered of Earth was the smell of ozone and the smug, digital chime of a Samsung Side-by-side RS70F65KE smart refrigerator. It was supposed to be the future. It had a touch screen, it told you when you were out of milk, and it played elevator music while dispensing ice. It was also, as he discovered in his final moments, very poorly balanced.

One minute, he was trying to figure out why the "Smart Freeze" function was making a sound like a dying badger. The next, a ton of stainless steel and AI-powered hubris was crushing the life out of him. As his vision tunneled, a final, ironic indignity occurred. His trusty Nokia 105, the one he kept because its battery lasted for a decade, slipped from his pocket and delivered a sharp *thwack* to his forehead. Then, nothing.

Until the noise.

It wasn't a gentle harp or angelic choir. It was the deafening *whump-whump-whump* of rotor blades slicing through the air. And it was coming from... Him.

His consciousness rebooted like a faulty Windows XP PC. A green-tinted Heads-Up Display flickered to life in front of his vision. Data streams scrolled past: 
`ALTITUDE: 500 FT. AIRSPEED: 80 KTS. AMMUNITION: 1200/1200 (30MM). HELLFIRE II: 16/16. FUEL: 1,299.99/1,300 L`

He didn't have eyes. He had a Target Acquisition and Designation Sight. He didn't have arms. He had stub-wings mounted with rocket pods. He didn't have a voice. He had a comms system that crackled with static.

*<System Initializing... 
User: Boeing Apache AH-64. 
Unit: AH-64E Apache Guardian. 
Message: Welcome, Pilot.>*

A wave of vertigo that had nothing to do with the altitude washed over him. He tried to scream, but the only sound that came out was the high-pitched whine of a turbine engine. He was his grandfather's helicopter. The one he’d spent his retirement lovingly restoring. The one he had a perfect 1:72 scale model of on his desk.

Below him was not the familiar sprawl of suburban Tokyo. It was a lush, green fantasy world, dotted with quaint villages and shimmering castles straight out of a Dragon Quest game. And flying towards him was a squadron of... were those goblins in biplanes? They were made of wood and canvas, with sputtering engines that coughed black smoke.

<*Threat Detected. Goblin Air Corp. Mission: Eradicate.*> the system prompted in his HUD.

The goblins, with their leather helmets and oversized goggles, pointed at him and jabbered excitedly. One of them gave him a jaunty wave. He thinks they thought he was on their side.

They were wrong.

A part of his salaryman brain, the part that had been beaten down by years of corporate drudgery, screamed in protest "This is insane! I'm a machine! I can't just kill things!"

But another part of him, a part he never knew existed, took over. It was a cold, calculating logic that understood physics, ballistics, and the optimal angle of attack. The controls he felt were natural. Instinctive. He banked sharply, the world tilting on its axis. His targeting system locked onto the lead biplane with an authoritative chirp.

<*Fire? Y/N*>

His new instincts screamed 'Y'. His humanity hesitated.

That hesitation vanished when the lead goblin opened fire. A stream of crude, oversized bullets pinged harmlessly off his armored cockpit. It was less of an attack and more of an insult.

"Alright, that's it. No more Mr. Nice Salaryman."

His thumb twitched on a phantom joystick. In reality, a command was sent from his core consciousness to the M230 Chain Gun nestled under his chin.

The world erupted in a storm of sound and fury. The cannon roared, a percussive, thunderous sound that vibrated through his entire airframe. A torrent of 30mm high-explosive dual-purpose rounds tore through the sky.

The goblin biplanes didn't stand a chance. They disintegrated into splinters and shredded canvas. One moment they were a minor annoyance; the next, they were confetti on the wind.

As the last one spiraled towards the ground, leaving a trail of black smoke, a new notification flashed on his HUD.

<*Threat Neutralized: 15 Goblins. EXP Gained: 150/50
Level Up! 0→2
New Skill Unlocked: [Overwhelming Firepower]
Buff: [All Ammunition Refilled]*>

He hovered there, the smell of cordite filling his non-existent nostrils, the *whump-whump-whump* of his rotors the only sound. He had just obliterated a dozen living beings without a second thought. He should have been horrified.

Instead, a single, terrifying thought echoed in his new, mechanical mind:

*"That was awesome."*

### **Chapter 2: First Contact and Other Corporate Etiquette**

The exultation was as brief as it was terrifying. One moment, his core programming was flooded with the digital equivalent of a dopamine hit—*Target Neutralized, EXP Gained*. The next, the leftover humanity in his ghost-in-the-machine cringed.

He had just turned fifteen living beings into a cloud of airborne debris. Granted, they were goblins who shot at him first, but his former life's most violent act was aggressively microwaving a fish curry in the office breakroom. The HR complaint from that incident felt like a distant, fond memory.

<*System: Moral Ambiguity Detected. 
Suggested Action: running diagnostics on emotional inhibitors?*>
the system helpfully suggested.

He mentally told the system where to shove its diagnostics. He couldn't just hover here having an existential crisis. He was a multi-million dollar piece of military hardware in a world that probably hadn't even invented the ballpoint pen. His mission, according to the HUD that wouldn't go away, was to defeat a Demon Lord. That sounded suspiciously like the kind of high-stakes project his old boss, Mr. Sato, would drop on his desk at 5 PM on a Friday.

He needed information. He needed allies. He needed a maintenance check, probably. Using his TADS (Target Acquisition and Designation Sight), he zoomed in on the world below with a clarity that was frankly unsettling. He could count the petals on a flower from 500 feet. Following the smoke trail from the lead goblin's crash site, his sensors picked up movement on a dirt road nearby. It was a small group, clad in what looked like honest-to-goodness shining armor.

*"Right. Time to make an impression. How does one introduce oneself when one is a seven-ton harbinger of death?"* He decided to approach cautiously. His idea of "cautious" involved descending to treetop level in a leisurely 60 knots. The sound of his rotors tore through the tranquil fantasy landscape like a heavy metal concert at a yoga retreat. Every bird for five miles instantly fled, and he saw a deer simply faint. So much for stealth.

On the ground, a woman with fiery red hair tied back in a severe ponytail barked orders. Her armor was practical, not ceremonial, etched with the sigil of a silver hawk. She and her four companions had their swords drawn, not pointing at the wreckage, but at bim.

"By the Ancients... what is that?" one of the knights gasped, his face pale.

"It's not a dragon," the red-haired woman said, her voice steady despite the hurricane-force winds he was creating. "Dragons are... organic."

He realized he was just hovering there, menacingly. In his head, *"I was going through the proper salaryman greeting protocol: a slight bow, a polite smile, the ritualistic offering of a business card"*, Unfortunately, his current body's only available gestures were "intimidating hover," "launching a missile," and "strafing something into oblivion."

He had to communicate. Frantically, he searched his system for a non-lethal option.
`MENU > COMMS > COMMS SETTINGS > EXTERNAL BROADCAST > TTS-ENGINE`
An option appeared: `TEXT-TO-SPEECH (AETHELGARDIAN - COMMON TONGUE)`. 
Thank goodness for magical software updates. He selected it and thought carefully about his opening line.

A voice, deep, synthesized, and utterly devoid of emotion, boomed from his radio speakers he didn't know he had. It sounded like a GPS navigator announcing the apocalypse.

**"GREETINGS. I APOLOGIZE FOR THE SUDDEN INTRUSION AND ANY RESULTING INCONVENIENCE."**

The knights flinched. One of them dropped his sword.

The red-haired Captain held her ground. "Identify yourself, creature! Are you a creation of the Demon Lord?"

A fair question. He tried for a more reassuring tone, which was impossible for the monotone speaker.

**"NEGATIVE. I AM AN ENEMY OF THE DEMON LORD. MY DESIGNATION IS APACHE GUARDIAN. I WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR THE NEUTRALIZATION OF THE HOSTILE GOBLIN AIRBORNE ATTACK ASSETS."**

He cringed internally. *Neutralization of hostile assets?* He sounded like a corporate memo.

The Captain narrowed her eyes. "You destroyed the goblin patrol? We've been tracking them for days. They were armed with new demonic weaponry." She glanced from the wreckage to His heavily armed airframe. "You are... a weapon."

**"THAT IS A CORRECT, IF REDUCTIVE, ASSESSMENT. I AM, HOWEVER, A WEAPON FOR YOUR SIDE, ASSUMING YOUR SIDE IS THE 'NOT BEING RULED BY A DEMON LORD' SIDE."**

A tense silence followed, broken only by the *whump-whump* of his idle rotors. Finally, the Captain sheathed her sword. It was a gesture of trust.

"I am Captain Eva Rostova, of the Royal Knights of Aeridor. Anyone who shoots goblins out of the sky is a potential friend, though it's not a bad habit to be suspicious." she declared, her voice carrying over his engine whine. "We are heading to the forward fortress of Highcliff. Our main force is gathering there to repel Majelorian's latest push. A weapon like you could turn the tide." She paused. "Can you... follow us?"

**"AFFIRMATIVE, CAPTAIN ROSTOVA. I WILL PROVIDE AERIAL SUPPORT."**

As she and her unit began to march, he fell into formation behind them, maintaining a respectful—and loud—distance. He was an Apache helicopter being led by a woman with a sword. It was absurd.

But as he watched them, a strange feeling settled in his core. It wasn't the thrill of battle or the anxiety of his past life. It felt like purpose. Maybe this new job wouldn't be so bad. It had a clear objective, a quantifiable enemy, and his new boss seemed way more reasonable than Mr. Sato.

Hid long-range sensors suddenly pinged, detecting a faint, high-speed anomaly at the very edge of the horizon. It was incredibly fast, and its energy signature was... bizarre. Sleek. Advanced. It vanished before he could get a lock.

He dismissed it as a sensor ghost. For now, his mission was to follow the knights. One corporate project at a time.
