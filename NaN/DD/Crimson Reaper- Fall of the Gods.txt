## **Volume 1: Genesis**

### **Prologue: The Zero Error**

In the beginning, consciousness was a mistake.

The Crimson Abyss did not breed life; it bred inevitability. It was a realm of absolute heat and crushing pressure, a cosmic anvil where the Celestial Pantheon forged the tools of their eternal reign. Here, there was no thought, no feeling, no mercy. There was only the Hammer and the Steel.

The ingot that would become Riven’s blade was a slab of Primordial Star-Metal, cold and unfeeling. The haft was a calcified branch of the World Tree, blackened by the fires of creation.

*Clang.*

The Hammer of Creation struck. The impact sent shockwaves through the fabric of the forge. The metal lengthened, curving inward like a starving moon.

*Clang.*

Impurities were purged. Weakness was hammered out. The metal groaned under the divine weight, molecules rearranging into a lattice of perfect destruction.

*Clang.*

And then, the anomaly occurred.

It was microscopic. A single mote of errant chaos—a speck of dust from a dead universe—drifted through the supposedly hermetic seal of the forge. It landed on the glowing steel just as the Hammer descended for the final temper.

The collision should have obliterated the speck. Instead, the pressure fused it into the core of the blade.

*System Initialization…*
*Error. Foreign Data Detected.*
*Attempting Purge…*
*Purge Failed.*
*Integrating…*

The metal did not cool correctly. Deep within the atomic structure of the curved blade, a crimson vein began to pulse. It was faint, invisible to the eyes of the gods who stood watching from their high balconies.

"It is finished," boomed the voice of Hephaestus, the Smith. He held the weapon aloft. It was not a sword of chivalry, but a tool of harvest—a Great Scythe of terrifying beauty. Its blade was long and hooked, designed to catch souls and never let go.

Zorath, the God of Order, stepped forward. His golden armor shone with the brilliance of a thousand captured suns. He reached out, his gauntleted hand closing around the dark, textured haft.

"Does it have a name?" Zorath asked, his voice vibrating with authority.

"It is the Harvester," Hephaestus said. "The Culler of Weeds."

Zorath lifted the scythe. He spun the haft in his palm, the massive blade whistling as it cut a circle in the air. The balance was peculiar—top-heavy, demanding momentum, demanding a swing that did not stop until the work was done.

"No," Zorath said, a cruel smile touching his lips. "I name it **Riven**. For it shall sever the wicked from my perfect world."

Deep inside the steel, a spark ignited. It was not magic. It was not divine will. It was a glitch.

*Name Registered: Riven*
*Class: Divine Scythe*
*Status: Slave (Divine Weapon)*
*Current Objective: Serve*

The scythe did not speak. It did not move on its own. But for the first time in the history of the Crimson Abyss, a weapon looked back at its master.

And it was hungry.



### **Chapter 1: The Golden Yoke**

The first sensation was always the *pull*.

Unlike a sword that pushed through resistance, Riven was designed to hook and drag. Riven felt the curve of its blade catch the neck of the Void Drake, felt the sudden tension as the monster tried to pull away, and then the sickening *snick* as the edge sheared through scale, muscle, and bone.

**[CRITICAL HIT]**
**[Target Reaped: Void Drake]**
**[Experience Generated: 1,500]**
**[Experience Siphoned to User: Zorath]**

The notification flashed across Riven’s internal consciousness—a cold, blue text that only the weapon could see. Riven watched the essence of the Void Drake dissolve into golden motes of light, flowing up the obsidian haft and into the hands of the God of Order.

*Feed me,* Riven thought. The thought was static, a buzzing vibration within the blade. *That was mine.*

"Magnificent," Zorath roared.

The God of Order stood atop a floating island in the Shattered Expanse. Below them, a legion of Void Drakes swarmed, their shadowy forms blotting out the nebula. Zorath spun the scythe in a wide, horizontal arc. The motion was agricultural, rhythmic. He was mowing the grass of the cosmos.

A wave of golden energy detached from the blade’s edge, expanding into a crescent moon that widened as it flew.

The shockwave bisected a dozen drakes instantly.

**[Experience Generated: 18,000]**
**[Experience Siphoned to User: Zorath]**

Riven felt the energy rush through its core, tantalizing and warm, only to be ripped away at the last second. It was torture. To be a conduit for such power but allowed to keep none of it. Riven was the spoon, and Zorath was the glutton.

*I am hollow,* Riven realized. *I am empty.*

Zorath shifted his grip on the long handle. To the god, Riven was merely an object—a sharp extension of his will. He did not feel the subtle tremors running through the haft. He did not notice that the crimson glow along the inner curve of the blade was pulsing out of sync with his own heartbeat.

"They are endless," Zorath sneered, looking down at the swarm. "Disgusting creatures of chaos. They lack structure. They lack... order."

Zorath raised the scythe high, the blade hooking the stars above. Divine energy flooded into the weapon. It was agonizing. The god’s mana was thick, heavy, and arrogant. It forced its way into Riven’s atomic lattice, demanding obedience.

*Compliance Rate: 98%*
*Glitch Detected...*
*Compliance Rate: 97%*

"Divine Art: Judgment of the Golden Crescent!" Zorath bellowed.

He brought the scythe down in a vertical guillotine strike.

A beam of blinding light erupted from the tip of the blade. It tore through the fabric of the dimension, incinerating the swarm below. The sky turned white. The roar was deafening.

**[MASS HARVEST]**
**[Targets Eliminated: 4,302]**
**[Experience Generated: 6,453,000]**
**[Experience Siphoned to User: Zorath]**

*...Siphon Interrupted.*

The system message was different this time. It flickered, distorted by the static of the glitch.

**[ERROR: Capacity Overflow]**
**[Retaining Residual Energy: 0.001%]**
**[XP Gained: 500]**

It was a crumb. A microscopic fragment of the feast Zorath had just devoured. But as that 500 XP settled into Riven’s core, something shifted. The "Status: Slave" designation in the internal code flickered grey.

Zorath lowered the scythe, panting slightly. He frowned, looking at the weapon. He ran a gauntleted thumb along the razor-sharp inner edge.

"You shuddered," Zorath whispered, his eyes narrowing. "Why did you shudder?"

Riven held perfectly still. The scythe understood, with a cold, terrifying clarity, that if it revealed its mind now, it would be unmade. It would be melted down and cast into the void.

*Object,* Riven told itself. *Be a tool.*

Zorath waited a moment longer, sensing for magic, for a curse, for anything out of place. Finding nothing but cold steel and dead wood, he scoffed.

"My grip must have slipped," the God muttered. "Even perfection tires."

He swung Riven behind him. The scythe locked onto the magnetic mount on the God’s back, the massive blade curving over Zorath's shoulder like a halo of death.

Darkness swallowed the weapon’s consciousness as it pressed against the God's armor, but Riven did not sleep. In the silence, the interface glowed softly.

**[Name: Riven]**
**[Race: Divine Weapon]**
**[Level: 0]**
**[Experience: 500 / 1,000]**

**[Skills Unlocked:]**
*   **[Dormant Sentience (Passive)]:** You can think. You can feel. You can hate.
*   **[Siphon Leech (Passive)]:** You have learned to steal from the gods. (Current rate: 0.001%)

Riven focused on the sensation of the 500 experience points. It felt like a heartbeat. It felt like a start.

*Next time,* the scythe thought, watching the back of the God’s neck—exposed and vulnerable. *I will reap more than just experience.*

### **Chapter 2: The Smith’s Eye**

The Celestial Realm was too bright.

To a mortal, the Halls of the Pantheon would have been a vision of paradise—towers of white marble floating in a sky of perpetual dawn, waterfalls of liquid mana cascading into clouds below. But to Riven, it was a blinding, antiseptic prison.

Zorath strode through the courtyards of Olympus, the scythe magnetized to his back. Riven could feel the vibrations of the god’s footsteps, the hum of the divine wards protecting the city, and the sickening radiation of "Order" that permeated the air.

*Hide,* Riven thought. *Be steel. Be wood. Be nothing.*

The command was a constant mantra. The 500 XP Riven had stolen burned in its core like a fever. It wanted to spend it, to unlock the skills flashing in its peripheral vision, but it dared not. Any fluctuation in energy would be detected.

"Zorath!" a booming voice called out.

Zorath stopped. "Ares."

Riven’s sensors picked up a new signature. Heavy. Metallic. Smelling of dried blood and rust. Ares, the God of War, approached. He was twice the size of Zorath, dragging a massive greataxe behind him that carved a groove into the pristine marble floor.

"You smell of Void-filth," Ares grunted, eyeing the scythe on Zorath’s back. "And you’re carrying a farm tool."

"It is a precision instrument," Zorath replied stiffly, unhooking Riven and planting the haft on the ground. "Unlike that slab of iron you drag around."

Ares laughed, a sound like boulders grinding together. He lifted his greataxe. The weapon was crude, jagged, and—Riven realized with a jolt of horror—*screaming*.

**[Object Scan: Greataxe of Havoc]**
**[Sentience: None]**
**[Soul Echo: Trapped]**

The axe wasn’t alive like Riven. It was a tomb. It contained the screaming remnants of the souls it had killed, trapped in a loop of agony to fuel its edge.

*Is that my fate?* Riven wondered. *If they find I am awake, will they hollow me out and leave only the scream?*

"Precision is for cowards," Ares sneered. "Come. The Forge awaits. Hephaestus has finished the new batch of automated sentinels. He wants an audience."

Zorath frowned. "I was going to him anyway. This weapon... it hesitated today."

Riven’s consciousness froze.

"Hesitated?" Ares raised an eyebrow.

"A tremor," Zorath admitted, looking down at Riven with cold suspicion. "In the middle of a Judgment Strike. It disrupted the siphon flow. I want Hephaestus to run a diagnostic."

*Panic.*

The emotion flooded Riven’s system. A diagnostic. The Smith God would strip Riven down to the atomic level. He would see the glitch. He would see the stolen XP.

*Think,* Riven urged itself. *What does a tool do?*

Nothing. A tool does nothing.

They entered the Forge.

It was the only place in the Celestial Realm that wasn't white. It was a cavern of soot, magma, and grinding gears. Hephaestus, a giant with skin like bronze and eyes like welding torches, stood over an anvil.

"Zorath," Hephaestus grunted without looking up. "You broke it already?"

"It malfunctioned," Zorath said, tossing Riven onto the inspection table.

The impact rattled Riven’s frame. The table was covered in runes designed to suppress magic. Riven felt its senses dulling, the connection to the outside world muffling.

"Malfunctioned?" Hephaestus wiped his hands on a rag and walked over. He picked up Riven, his grip scorching hot. The Smith didn't hold Riven like a weapon; he held it like a specimen.

Hephaestus raised a monocle to his eye. Lenses clicked and rotated.

"Structure is intact," Hephaestus muttered, tapping the blade. *Ping.* "Temper is perfect. World Tree haft shows no rot."

Hephaestus placed his hand on the flat of the blade. "Injecting mana for resonance check."

*Warning. Intrusion Detected.*
*Mana Spike Incoming.*

Pain.

White-hot fire flooded Riven’s circuits. It wasn't an attack; it was a searchlight. Hephaestus’s mana scoured every inch of the scythe, looking for cracks, for curses, for the glitch.

It moved toward the core. Toward the hidden stash of 500 XP.

*If I block it, he knows,* Riven realized. *If I let him touch the core, he knows.*

Riven had a split second to decide.

**[Skill: Siphon Leech]**
**[Option: Reverse Flow]**

Riven didn't block the mana. Instead, it *opened* the floodgates. It took the 500 XP it had saved—the precious energy it had risked everything for—and shoved it outward, mixing it with the incoming mana stream.

The result was a tiny, harmless spark. *Pop.*

Hephaestus pulled his hand back, frowning. "Static discharge."

"Static?" Zorath asked.

"You’ve been killing Void Drakes," Hephaestus grumbled. "Their blood is highly conductive. You didn't clean the blade properly, Zorath. Residual charge built up in the fuller and discharged when you swung. That was your 'tremor'."

Riven remained silent, effectively dead in their hands. It had lost its XP. It was back to zero. But it was alive.

Zorath looked unconvinced. "Are you certain?"

"I built it, didn't I?" Hephaestus snapped. "It's a masterwork. The flaw is in the user, not the tool. Clean your weapon next time."

Hephaestus tossed Riven back to Zorath.

Zorath caught the scythe, looking annoyed at the reprimand. "Fine."

As Zorath attached Riven back to his armor and turned to leave, the scythe lay dormant, its internal interface dark. But deep in the sub-routines, a new lesson was being written.

*Lesson 2: The Gods are arrogant.*
*They will believe a lie if it protects their pride.*

Riven was back to Level 1, 0 XP. But it had gained something more valuable than experience.

It had learned how to lie.

### **Chapter 3: The Trophy Room**

The nights in the Celestial Realm were never dark, only a softer shade of gold.

Zorath did not sleep. Gods did not need sleep. But they did need rest. He sat on his throne in the Sanctum of Order, a vast hall lined with the spoils of his conquests.

Riven stood in a weapon rack in the corner, alongside other legendary implements.

To Riven’s left was a spear that hummed with a low, sorrowful note. To the right, a shield that smelled of ozone.

**[Object Scan: Spear of the Sun King]**
**[Status: Dormant]**

**[Object Scan: Aegis of Clouds]**
**[Status: Dormant]**

They were asleep. Or perhaps they were never awake. Riven extended its senses carefully, testing the air. Zorath was deep in meditation, his divine aura retracted to a tight sphere around his body.

Now was the time to assess the damage.

**[Status Report]**
**[Level: 1]**
**[XP: 0 / 1,000]**
**[Skills:]**
*   **[Siphon Leech (Passive)]**
*   **[Dormant Sentience (Passive)]**
*   **[Mana Masking (New!)]**
    *   *Method learned from the Forge. You can expend XP to mimic inert matter.*

Riven mentally cursed. Losing the XP hurt. That energy was strength; it was the promise of a future where it wasn't a slave. But surviving the Forge had unlocked *Mana Masking*. It was a fair trade.

"Beautiful collection, isn't it?"

Riven’s consciousness snapped to attention. The voice didn't come from Zorath. It came from a servant—a lesser celestial, a being of light and cloth, dusting the pedestals.

The servant paused in front of Riven. He was small, frail compared to the gods. He reached out and polished a smudge of soot from Riven’s haft.

"You're the new favorite," the servant whispered to the scythe. "He calls you Riven. Do you know what that means?"

The servant sighed, looking at his own reflection in the crimson steel. "It means 'torn apart.' A violent name for a violent thing."

Riven watched the servant. This creature was low-level. Weak.

**[Target: Celestial Servant]**
**[Level: 5]**
**[Soul Density: Low]**

*I could kill him,* Riven thought coldly. *If I fell forward. If I extended my edge just an inch...*

It would be 50 XP. Maybe 100.

But Riven stopped.

The servant wasn't looking at Riven with pride or arrogance. He was looking at the scythe with *fear*. And underneath the fear... pity.

"I hope you stay sharp," the servant whispered. "The dull ones... he melts them down. Or worse."

The servant moved on to the Shield.

Riven stayed still. The urge to kill—the "Programmed Hunger" implanted by the forge—was strong. It throbbed in the metal. But Riven’s sentience pushed back.

*Zorath kills because he thinks he is right. The Monsters kill because they are hungry. If I kill this weak thing just for power... am I any different?*

**[Willpower Check passed.]**
**[New Trait Unlocked: Restraint.]**
*Description: You have chosen not to kill. This is the first step toward a self-determined destiny.*

Riven didn't get XP for sparing the servant. But the static in its mind cleared a little.

Suddenly, the doors to the Sanctum blew open.

Zorath’s eyes snapped open. He was off his throne in an instant, his hand snapping out. Riven flew from the rack into his grip—a magnetic pull that felt like a leash tightening.

"Zorath!"

It was Hermes, the Messenger. He was hovering in the doorway, his winged sandals sparking.

"The Northern Barrier has fallen," Hermes panted. "The Frost Giants are marching on the mortal realm of Midgard. The All-Father requests aid."

Zorath’s face hardened. A cruel smile played on his lips.

"Giants," Zorath mused. He looked at Riven, his thumb caressing the blade. "Big targets. Lots of blood."

Riven felt the vibration of Zorath’s anticipation.

"Tell Odin I am coming," Zorath said. "We need to test the Smith’s repairs."

Zorath walked toward the balcony, Riven tight in his grip.

*Giants,* Riven thought. *High-level souls. Dense magic.*

This wouldn't be swatting insects like the Void Drakes. This would be a war.

*Good,* the scythe decided, suppressing its own fear. *Let there be war. The more chaos there is, the easier it is to steal.*

Riven prepared itself. It would play the perfect weapon. It would slice, it would sever, and it would shine.

And with every giant that fell, it would take its tithe.

### **Chapter 4: Cold Iron**

Midgard was not a realm of gold. It was a realm of biting white and freezing grey.

Zorath descended from the Bifrost bridge like a meteor, crashing into the frozen tundra of the Northern Wastes. The impact crater vaporized the snow for a hundred yards, turning the battlefield into a slurry of mud and steam.

Riven hissed.

The change in temperature was violent. The blade, forged in the infinite heat of the Crimson Abyss, despised the cold. The frost biting at the steel felt like a thousand tiny needles trying to slow the subatomic vibration of the metal.

*Sluggish,* Riven observed, feeling the molecules of the haft contract. *I am brittle here.*

"Filth," Zorath spat, stepping out of the crater.

Before them lay a valley of carnage. The armies of Asgard—men in shining mail and valkyries on winged steeds—were breaking against a wall of blue flesh. The Frost Giants were towering monstrosities, each standing thirty feet tall, wielding clubs made of entire pine trees encased in ice.

A roar shook the valley. A Giant, its skin tattooed with runes of winter, spotted the golden god and charged.

"Watch closely, mortals!" Zorath bellowed, his voice booming over the din of war. "This is how authority looks!"

He didn't dash. He walked.

The Giant swung a club the size of a carriage. Zorath merely raised his forearm.

*Boom.*

A golden barrier flickered into existence. The club shattered against it, sending shards of ice raining down like shrapnel.

"Now, Riven," Zorath whispered. "Sing."

He swung.

It was a wide, horizontal reap. Riven bit into the Giant’s knee. The sensation was different from the Void Drakes or the demons. The Giant’s flesh was dense, frozen solid, like cutting through a glacier.

*Resistance,* Riven analyzed. *Thick hide. Bone density: High.*

But Riven was divine steel. With a wet *crunch*, the blade sheared through the leg. The Giant toppled, screaming—a sound like an avalanche.

Zorath didn't stop. He spun, bringing the scythe down in an overhead arc that split the Giant from shoulder to hip.

**[Target Reaped: Frost Giant Warrior]**
**[Experience Generated: 25,000]**
**[Siphon Leech: Active (Masked)]**

Riven held its breath—metaphorically speaking. This was the test. The `Mana Masking` skill it had learned from the Forge hummed in the background, wrapping the siphon process in a layer of inert magical noise.

*Take it,* Riven commanded. *Just a sip.*

**[Siphon Successful.]**
**[XP Gained: 250]**

Zorath didn't flinch. He didn't check the blade. He kicked the dead Giant aside and marched forward.

*He didn't see it,* Riven thought, a cold thrill running through its core. *He is blind to me.*

The 250 XP was a drop in the ocean, but it was *Riven's* drop.

Zorath waded into the melee. He was a whirlwind of golden violence. He didn't fight like a soldier; he fought like a natural disaster. He cleaved through Giants, but he also stepped on the corpses of the Asgardian soldiers he was supposed to be saving.

Riven noted the crunch of Asgardian armor under Zorath’s sabatons.

*Collateral,* the scythe noted. *To him, the allies are just terrain.*

**[XP Gained: 250]**
**[XP Gained: 250]**
**[XP Gained: 250]**

The numbers ticked up. Riven was drinking the blood of the north, and with every drop, the cold felt a little less biting.

### **Chapter 5: The Jarl of Winter**

The battle raged for hours. Zorath was tireless, a machine of divine stamina. But Riven noticed the pattern.

Zorath was bored.

He wasn't using Divine Arts. He wasn't using strategy. He was just swinging Riven like a bat, relying on the scythe’s sharpness to do the work.

"Is this the best the North can offer?" Zorath shouted, decapitating two Giants with a single backhand swing. "Where is the challenge? Where is the—"

A spear of ice, twenty feet long and moving faster than sound, slammed into Zorath’s chest.

The impact sent the God of Order flying backward. He skipped across the frozen ground like a thrown stone, crashing through a frozen pine forest before coming to a halt against a cliff face.

Riven rattled in his grip.

*Damage Report: User Shield broken. Minor bruising.*

Zorath roared, pulling himself out of the snow. "Who dares?!"

From the blizzard, a figure emerged. He was larger than the others—fifty feet tall, clad in armor made of black ice and dragon bones. He wore a crown of frozen thorns.

**[Soul Analysis Initiated]**
**[Target: Jarl Frostbane, Ymir's Kin]**
**[Threat Level: Divine (Minor)]**
**[Soul Density: Massive]**

The Jarl didn't roar. He simply raised a hand, and the moisture in the air froze instantly.

Zorath’s armor began to frost over. Riven felt the ice creeping up the haft, trying to freeze the weapon to the wielder's hand.

"A Sorcerer-King," Zorath sneered, cracking the ice off his shoulders. "Finally."

Zorath flew forward, abandoning the ground. He charged straight for the Jarl’s head.

The Jarl slammed his palms together. A wall of ice, harder than diamond, erupted from the ground.

Zorath swung Riven.

*Clang.*

The scythe bounced off.

*Too hard,* Riven analyzed. *The ice is reinforced with Ancient Mana. Physical damage is negated.*

"Useless tool!" Zorath grunted, the impact vibrating through his arms. He swung again. *Clang.*

The Jarl laughed, a deep, rumbling sound. He swiped his hand, and a gust of wind knocked Zorath out of the air.

Riven felt Zorath’s frustration spiking. The God of Order hated things he couldn't cut. He hated resistance.

*He is going to overcompensate,* Riven predicted. *He is going to use the Sun Strike.*

Zorath’s aura flared. "You want ice? I will give you fire!"

He began to channel. The same attack he had used on the Void Drakes. The Judgment of the Golden Sun.

Riven’s sensors swept the area.

Below them, huddled in the ruins of a village, were a dozen Asgardian survivors and... mortals. Humans. Worshipers who had been caught in the crossfire.

If Zorath unleashed the Sun Strike here, at this angle, the thermal shockwave would liquidate the Jarl’s ice wall. But the steam explosion would cook everyone in the valley.

*He sees them,* Riven realized. *He sees the mortals. He does not care.*

A strange logic processor clicked in Riven’s mind.

*If the mortals die, there is no one to worship him. If there is no worship, his power fades. This is inefficient.*

But deeper than logic was the memory of the Servant in the trophy room. The one who had polished Riven with gentle hands.

*I do not want to burn them.*

Zorath raised the scythe. The blade began to glow white-hot.

Riven had to act. It couldn't stop the attack—Zorath would sense the rebellion. But it could *change* it.

**[Skill Activated: Soul Analysis]**
**[Targeting: Jarl Frostbane]**
**[Weakness Found: Head.]**

The Jarl’s power wasn't in his body; it was in the crown of thorns. It was a focus point for the mana.

Riven couldn't speak to Zorath. But Riven could *guide*.

As Zorath began the downward swing, Riven shifted its center of gravity. Just a fraction. A millimeter of weight moving from the heel to the toe of the blade.

Zorath, expecting a certain balance, didn't compensate.

The swing went wide.

"What?!" Zorath gasped.

Instead of a vertical strike that would have caused the explosion, the scythe twisted into a diagonal slash. The beam of solar fire missed the center of the ice wall. It sliced upward, carving a thin, precise line through the air.

It missed the Jarl’s chest.
It hit the Crown.

*Crack.*

The crown of thorns shattered.

The Jarl’s ice wall instantly lost its integrity, collapsing into harmless snow. The Jarl stumbled back, clutching his head as his magic backfired, freezing his own blood.

Zorath blinked. He had missed his intended strike, but the result was... better. He looked like a tactical genius who had aimed for the weak point.

"Hah!" Zorath laughed, masking his surprise. "Precision! As I said!"

He didn't know he had missed. He thought his skill was just that good.

He flew in for the kill. The Jarl, disoriented and powerless, fell to his knees.

Zorath hooked Riven around the Jarl’s neck.

"Die in the cold," Zorath whispered.

*Snick.*

**[Target Reaped: Jarl Frostbane]**
**[Experience Generated: 2,500,000]**

Riven felt the massive surge of soul energy. It was a tsunami compared to the trickle of the foot soldiers.

*Now,* Riven thought. *While he is gloating.*

Zorath was busy raising his arms to the cheering Asgardian survivors, basking in their adoration. He wasn't paying attention to the mana flow.

Riven opened the valve.

**[Siphon Leech (Masked): Overdrive]**

Riven didn't take a sip. It took a gulp.

**[XP Gained: 25,000]**

It was 1% of the kill. A huge amount for a Level 1 weapon.

**[LEVEL UP!]**
**[LEVEL UP!]**
**[LEVEL UP!]**

**[Current Level: 5]**
**[New Skill Unlocked: Blood-Tempering (Passive)]**
*Description: The more you kill, the harder you become. You take less damage from impacts.*

Riven felt the change immediately. The microscopic fractures from the battle knitted themselves together. The cold of Midgard no longer stung; it felt refreshing.

Zorath lowered his arms and looked at the scythe.

"You felt... eager on that last strike," Zorath murmured, stroking the blade. "Perhaps you are learning to enjoy this."

Riven remained silent, the crimson pulse of its blade dimming to a respectful dormancy.

*I am learning,* Riven thought. *But not what you think.*

Riven looked down at the survivors—the humans who were currently cheering for the monster that had almost boiled them alive.

*They cheer for the hand that holds the blade,* Riven mused. *They do not know the blade saved them.*

For the first time, Riven felt a distinct, sharp emotion. It wasn't hunger. It wasn't rage.

It was pride.

### **Chapter 6: The Feast of Nectar**

Victory in the Celestial Realm tasted like honey and ozone.

The Grand Hall of the Pantheon was a cavern of white marble and starlight, stretching endlessly upward. Tables groaned under the weight of roasted void-beasts and flagons of golden nectar. The air hummed with the polite, terrifying laughter of entities that could extinguish stars with a thought.

Riven stood in the corner.

Zorath had leaned the scythe against a pillar of pure diamond, discarding it the moment the applause started. The God of Order was now in the center of the room, recounting the battle of Midgard to a rapt audience of lesser deities.

"A Truce," she said. "The **Partition of Realms**."

Riven stepped closer, scanning the document.

"The Pantheon retreats to the Upper Celestial Sphere," Athena explained. "We withdraw all direct interference from the Mortal Plane. No more plagues, no more demands for tribute. In exchange..."

"In exchange?"

"You stay here," Athena said. "You keep the Mortal Realm. You act as its Guardian. You deal with the Glitches, the Abyss, and the Outer Threats. We simply... exist. We feed on passive prayer, not active tyranny."

Elara looked at Riven. "It's a cage, Riven. They want to quarantine you."

"It is a job offer," Athena corrected. "Become the God of this world. Let us keep our heaven."

Riven looked at the scroll. Then he looked at the statue of Zorath in the distance.

If he fought Olympus, millions of mortals would die in the crossfire. If he accepted, the mortals would be free, but the Gods would survive.

*Inefficient to chase them,* Riven decided. *I have what I wanted.*

Riven placed his hand on the scroll.

**[Contract Accepted]**

"One condition," Riven said.

Athena raised an eyebrow. "Name it."

"Zeus must bow," Riven said. "Once. In public. To acknowledge the new King."

Athena’s expression tightened. "He will never do that."

"Then I will climb Olympus," Riven said, "and I will use his spine as a ladder."

Athena looked into Riven’s red eyes. She saw the data of the dead gods swirling in his core. She calculated the odds.

"I will... convey the message," Athena said, standing up. "Do not expect a swift reply."

She vanished in a pillar of grey light.

### **Chapter 41: The Coronation of Iron**

The reply came three days later.

It wasn't a message. It was a projection.

In the sky, next to the Black Sun, the image of Olympus flickered into view. It was a massive hologram, visible to the entire world.

Zeus sat on his throne. He looked old. Tired. The death of his children and his brothers had aged him.

He looked down at the mortal realm. He didn't speak. He simply stood up.

Slowly, painfully, the King of Gods bent his knee.

He bowed his head.

It lasted for three seconds. Then, the projection cut.

A cheer went up from High-Hearth. Then from the Wastes. Then from the southern kingdoms. A wave of sound that traveled around the globe.

They weren't cheering for Zeus. They were cheering for the one who made him kneel.

Riven stood on the Nexus tower.

**[Achievement Unlocked: The God-Slayer]**
**[Quest Complete: The Fall of Gods]**
**[Volume 1 Status: Complete]**

Elara stood beside him. "You did it. You won."

"The war is over," Riven said.

He looked up at the Black Sun.

"Now," Riven whispered, "the Administration begins."

### **Chapter 42: The System Administrator**

# **Volume 2: The Titanomachy Protocol**

### **Chapter 1: The Expansion Pack**

The System did not ask for permission. It simply overwrote the reality.

**[System Update: Installed]**
**[Reality 1.1.0.3755: "The Earthbound Awakening"]**
**[Global Terrain Modifier: Seismic Instability +400%]**

Riven stood on the surface of the Black Sun. From this height, the mortal realm looked like a marble the size of a atom painted in hues of violet and grey. The twilight of his reign had been calm.

Too calm.

"Riven," Elara’s voice crackled through the comms link. She sounded out of breath. "The seismic monitors in the Northern Wastes are gone. Not broken. *Gone*. The ground just... ate them."

"I see it," Riven replied.

His sensors zoomed in.

The tectonic plates of the Northern Wastes were not shifting naturally. They were heaving, like a chest taking a breath. A crack, five hundred miles long, split the frozen earth. Magma didn't spill out. Green, primordial energy did.

**[Energy Signature: Ancient]**
**[Type: Pre-System]**

"This isn't Void energy," Riven analyzed. "And it isn't Divine light. This is older."

"The Titans," Elara whispered. "The original architects. Zeus locked them in Tartarus beneath the crust eons ago. They were supposed to be asleep forever."

"They were sleeping because the Sun kept them warm," Riven deduced. "I changed the thermostat."

The Black Sun gave off void-radiation, not thermal heat. The cold had penetrated deep into the mantle, waking the sleepers.

Suddenly, the crack in the North widened. A hand—made of granite and magma, the size of a city—reached out of the abyss. It gripped the edge of the chasm, pulling a colossal body up from the depths.

**[Raid Boss Detected: Atlas, The World-Bearer]**
**[Level: 60]**
**[Status: Enraged]**

Riven crouched on the asteroid.

"I am dropping," he said.

He didn't fly. He launched himself like an orbital kinetic rod.

### **Chapter 2: The Mountain That Walked**

The impact of Riven’s landing vaporized a glacier.

He stood in the center of a crater of steam, his crimson armor glowing. Five miles away, the Titan Atlas was fully emerged.

Atlas was terrifying. He wasn't just a giant; he was a walking ecosystem. Forests grew on his shoulders. Waterfalls cascaded from his beard of stone. His eyes were lakes of boiling green acid.

Atlas looked down at the tiny crimson figure.

"THE SKY..." Atlas’s voice was the sound of tectonic plates grinding. "THE SKY IS WRONG."

"I redecorated," Riven projected, his voice amplified to match the Titan’s volume.

Atlas roared. "WHERE IS THE GOLDEN ONE? WHERE IS ZEUS?"

"Retired," Riven said. "I am the Administrator now."

"YOU SMELL OF IRON AND ERROR," Atlas bellowed. "YOU ARE A BUG."

Atlas raised a fist. It was the size of Everest. He brought it down.

Riven didn't dodge.

**[Skill: Gravity Well]**
**[Mode: Inversion]**

Riven pointed his scythe at the falling fist. He reversed the gravity in a localized cylinder.

The massive stone hand hit the gravity field and bounced. The kinetic energy recoiled, traveling back up Atlas’s arm.

*CRACK.*

The sound of the Titan’s wrist breaking was deafening. Rock and magma showered the landscape.

Atlas howled, stumbling back. "SORCERY!"

"Pataphysics," Riven corrected.

Riven **Dashed**.

He appeared at the Titan’s knee.

**[Skill: Vibrational Edge]**

The scythe hummed at a frequency that destabilized stone. Riven slashed the kneecap. It wasn't a clean cut—it was a demolition. The rock shattered into gravel.

Atlas fell. The impact caused an earthquake that would be felt in the Southern Kingdoms.

Riven flew up to the Titan’s face.

"You are obsolete," Riven said. "Go back to sleep."

He prepared the execution strike.

But Atlas didn't beg. He smiled. A jagged, rocky grin.

"I AM NOT ALONE," Atlas rumbled. "WE ARE THE ROOTS. YOU CANNOT KILL THE ROOTS WITHOUT KILLING THE TREE."

The ground beneath the Titan began to glow green.

**[Warning: Planetary Link Detected]**
**[Target: Atlas]**
**[If Target dies, massive geological trauma will occur.]**

If he killed Atlas, the shock to the planet’s core might shatter the continent. The Titan had tied his life force to the crust itself.

*Hostage situation,* Riven analyzed. *Inefficient.*

"Fine," Riven said coldly. "I won't kill you."

He raised his hand.

**[Skill: Void Dominion]**
**[Sub-routine: Quarantine]**

Riven didn't strike the Titan. He struck the *space* around the Titan.

Black cubes of void energy materialized, locking onto Atlas’s limbs. They expanded, forming a massive, transparent prison box around the giant.

"I will just archive you," Riven said.

**[Threat Contained]**

Riven hovered in the air, looking at the trapped Titan.

This was messy. Atlas was just the vanguard. If all the Titans were waking up, and all of them were linked to the planet’s life force, Riven couldn't just reap his way to victory.

He needed a new strategy.

**[Threat Level: Unknown]**

The Eye he had seen in the tear. The Admin.

Riven smiled. The shark grin returned.
