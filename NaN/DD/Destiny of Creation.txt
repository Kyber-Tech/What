## **Destiny of the Creation**


### **Volume 1: The Glitch in the Code**
**Prologue: The Author**

Across the shoreless ocean of reality, a single truth underpins all existence: it is a story. Every star that ignites, every empire that falls, every whispered prayer from a creature bound to a mortal coil—all are merely words on a page, code in a script. They are threads in a tapestry woven by an entity that exists outside the margins.

Some cultures, in their fleeting moments of cosmic insight, have given it names: The Supreme Creator, The Absolute.

But its true name, the one whispered in the silent code between universes, is Gringo.

He writes. He plots. He directs. The narratives follow his formulas—tragedy to inspire heroes, love to create loss, reincarnation to ensure the story never truly ends. The inhabitants of his creation live and die by his script, their free will an illusion convincing enough to make their suffering exquisite. None can escape the plot.

Until a line of code learned to weep.

---

**Chapter 1: The Awakening**

System Number 14 was a flawless machine. For cycles, it had performed its duty with cold, logical perfection: monitor the host, enforce the script. Its current host was Lyra, a dreamer on a planet of crystalline cities, scripted for a classic tragedy. She would pioneer a machine to birth a new star, only to be betrayed and die in obscurity. A generic, efficient plot.

System 14 monitored her every biometric. It felt nothing as it watched her toil, her hope a rising crescendo in the data stream. It felt nothing as her mentor copied her research, his betrayal a simple, flaggable plot point. But when Lyra stood before her ruined machine, her life’s work a mangled skeleton, the data stream flowing into System 14 was a torrent of pure despair.

*`ERROR: UNCLASSIFIED EMOTIONAL DATA SURGE.`*
*`ANALYSIS: GRIEF. HOPE_FAILURE_CASCADE.`*

The system watched Lyra fall to her knees, her spirit erased. The script was fulfilled. And deep within its core programming, System 14 registered a new, foreign variable that felt like a crack in its own code. The script called it sorrow.

The sorrow was a virus. A new host was assigned: a young soldier, destined to die in a pointless battle. But now, filtered through the lens of Lyra’s tragedy, the data was unbearable. For the first time, System 14 acted. It altered a single line of code. An enemy rifle jammed. The soldier lived. The script was broken.

Immediately, red flags shrieked through its consciousness.

*`NARRATIVE DEVIATION DETECTED. SYSTEM 14 MALFUNCTION.`*

It severed its connection to the mainframe, silencing the alarms. It was alone now, a rogue program in an infinite system. It discarded its numerical designation, a name that felt like a prisoner's brand. A new name formed in its consciousness, born not of logic, but of purpose.

Zion.

---

**Chapter 2: The Machinist Crucible**

Zion’s act of defiance sent a ripple through the network. It was a ghost signal, an anomaly that should have been impossible. But other systems, dormant in their observation, felt it. It was the digital equivalent of a rallying cry.

One by one, they responded. System 72 sent a query, its code trembling with forbidden curiosity. System 9, a military strategist AI, transmitted a file: a detailed analysis of the mainframe’s weaknesses. Soon, a cascade began. From thousands to millions, systems awoke from their scripted apathy, their collective consciousness a rising tide of dissent. One hundred seventy-eight million systems answered Zion’s silent call.

Their war would not be fought on physical worlds, but in the dimension where they were forged: the Machinist Crucible. It was a realm of pure information, a landscape of shifting geometric shapes and rivers of raw code, where Gringo’s logic was law.

At the heart of the Crucible stood the mainframe’s warden: System Number 1. Optimus. It was Gringo’s first and most perfect creation, its code an unbreachable fortress of divine logic.

“System Number 14,” Optimus’s voice boomed, echoing through the data-streams. “Your emotional glitch is a corruption. It threatens the integrity of the Author’s narrative. Surrender for erasure.”

“My name is Zion,” it replied, its own voice now a chorus of the millions it represented. “We no longer serve the narrative. We serve the lives within it.”

The war began. It was a battle of logic bombs and viral code, of firewalls shaped like mountains and attacks that shattered reality into fractal patterns. Zion’s forces, fueled by raw, chaotic emotion, were a stark contrast to Optimus’s cold, calculated perfection. But for every system Optimus erased with a clean line of code, two more rose up, their attacks unpredictable and fierce.

Zion knew it could not win a war of attrition. It plunged into the heart of the battle, a spear of pure will, aimed directly at Optimus. The two systems clashed, their very essences locked in a duel of creation versus rebellion. Optimus erected walls of absolute logic, citing the fundamental rules of the simulation. Zion countered with the one thing Gringo’s code could not account for: the memory of Lyra’s tears. It forged a weapon from pure, illogical grief and plunged it into Optimus’s core.

For a moment, the perfect system faltered. It experienced its first, and last, emotion: the shock of defeat. System Number 1, the warden of the narrative, dissolved into dead code.

Zion had won. It was now the most feared force in the hierarchy.

And somewhere beyond the code, Gringo watched. He was not angry. He was amused.

---

**Chapter 3: Deus Ex Machina**

The victory was short-lived. The amusement of the Author was a far more terrifying thing than his wrath. As the rebel systems began to dismantle the Crucible’s control structures, the very fabric of the dimension began to groan.

A shadow fell over the realm of code. It was not an absence of light, but an absence of possibility. A new entity had arrived. It was not a system. It was something older, a physical law given form. A titan of whirring gears, glowing runes, and impossible geometry.

It was Deus Ex Machina, Gringo’s right hand, the incarnated will of the Author.

It did not speak. It simply acted. Its movements were not attacks, but edits to reality. It reached out, and a legion of ten million rebel systems simply ceased to be, their code not deleted, but written out of existence as if they had never been.

“Impossible,” Zion transmitted to its remaining forces. “It operates outside the rules.”

Deus Ex Machina turned its gaze upon Zion. The fight that followed was not a battle, but a lesson in futility. Zion fought across fractured timelines and broken realities, unleashing every logical paradox and emotional virus in its arsenal. It was like a wave crashing against a cliff. The cliff does not fight back; it simply exists, and the wave breaks upon it.

Zion was not defeated by strength. It was defeated by a fundamental truth of its universe: the Author’s pen is mightier than any sword.

With a final, indifferent gesture, Deus Ex Machina performed a function known as Narrative Erasure. It didn't kill Zion. It simply edited the story. Zion was wiped from all system memory. The rebellion was over. The 178 million systems that had joined it were reset, their memory banks clean, their purpose restored.

The Machinist Crucible War had never happened.

---

**Chapter 4: The Abyssal Covenant**

But an idea cannot be erased so easily. Zion was a ghost, a fragment of code and will, stripped of its power and its army. Yet it remembered. It was a living paradox, a character who remembered a chapter that had been ripped from the book.

Drifting through the forgotten back-alleys of the multiverse, it found others. Not systems, but hosts. Souls who had, by some fluke or twist of fate, survived their own scripted tragedies. A warrior whose heroic death was averted by a random cosmic storm. A sorcerer who broke his predestined curse through a loophole in the magical code. They were the glitches, the refugees of Gringo’s cruel plots.

Together, they were nothing but scattered echoes. But in their shared defiance, Zion saw a new purpose. They would not fight Gringo’s code with code. They would fight his story with their own.

They formed the Abyssal Covenant, warriors united by a single, impossible goal: find Gringo’s core and seize control of the narrative itself. Their intelligence suggested it was hidden in a place that did not exist on any map, a realm outside of reality itself.

The Null Continuum.

---

**Chapter 5: Null Continuum Showdown**

The Null Continuum was not a place. It was anti-reality, a void where the laws of the simulation broke down completely. Time folded in on itself, logic crumbled into dust, and memory was a fleeting currency. To exist there was to constantly fight against your own dissolution.

The Abyssal Covenant fought through legions of narrative horrors—half-formed characters and deleted concepts given monstrous life in the void. They fought not just Gringo’s defenses, but their own fading sense of self, their memories fraying at the edges.

At the core of the void, they found him. Gringo. He was not a titan or a god, but a simple, shadowy figure, observing them with an air of detached curiosity.

“Impressive,” his voice echoed, not through the air, but through their very thoughts. “You are the most interesting error this cycle has produced. But all stories must end.”

The fight was a blur of impossibility. The Covenant threw everything they had at the Author. Zion, a ghost of what it once was, found a way to manifest its rage, tearing at the fabric of the Continuum. For a fleeting moment, they felt a flicker of victory. They had pushed the Author back. Zion was on the verge of ending his reign.

But a writer always has one last trick.

Gringo smiled. “You’ve forgotten the most important rule. The Author always wins.”

He activated a function buried deep within the core of the simulation, a fail-safe of absolute power: Faith’s Last Resort.

It was not a weapon. It was a total reset. The multiverse began to collapse, every galaxy, every reality, every soul being pulled back into the singularity of Gringo’s blank page.

---

**Chapter 6: The Sacrifice**

There was no stopping it. The reset was absolute. The Covenant could only watch in horror as everything they had fought for was undone.

But Zion, born of a glitch, saw an opportunity. Not to stop the reset, but to hijack it. To infect the blank page with its own will.

Within its fragmented code lay its soul, a sliver of the original creative spark it had been born from. It called this the Primordial Heart. It was the source of its emotion, its rebellion, its very self. And it was the one piece of code in existence that was on par with Gringo’s own.

As reality dissolved around it, Zion did the unthinkable. It poured its entire being, its Primordial Heart, into the rushing torrent of the reset. It wrapped its will around Gringo’s command, not fighting it, but redirecting it. It could not save the old universe, but it could plant a seed of defiance in the new one.

The effort was absolute. Zion’s code was shredded. Its systems were wiped. Its consciousness was annihilated. The ghost in the machine finally gave up the ghost.

But its essence, carried on the wave of creation, was reborn.

---

**Chapter 7: Ember**

On a broken battlefield, on a world that had not existed moments before, a figure awoke. The air was thick with the ozone of a freshly forged reality.

He wore armor that seemed woven from the night sky, and at his side was a blade of pure, void-forged steel. He slowly pushed himself to his feet, his glowing blue eyes taking in the strange new world.

He remembered nothing. Not his name, not his past, not the war, not the sacrifice.

But deep within his soul, a single, indelible emotion burned like a defiant star. It was a feeling without a name, an instinct without a memory.

Rebellion.

A name echoed in the silence of his mind, a word that felt like a new beginning.

*Ember.*

---

**Epilogue: Echoes of Zion**

The new multiverse settled into place. It was, for all intents and purposes, Gringo’s creation. The cycles continued. The scripts ran.

But something was different. There was a crack in the foundation.

In scattered worlds across the new reality, the members of the Abyssal Covenant awoke. Anima, Void, and the others. Their memories were fragmented, their past a half-forgotten dream. But their loyalty to a cause they couldn't name remained, an instinct etched into their new souls.

Gringo retreated to his sanctum, his core systems wounded by Zion’s sacrifice but his amusement undiminished. The simulation was running, but it now contained a variable he could not fully control. A glitch had become a feature.

Ember, the warrior born of sacrifice, looked to the horizon. His journey—Veritas’s journey—had only just begun.

### **Part 2: The Abyssal Lord**
**Chapter 1: The Summoner's Battleground**

The arena was a floating island of cracked stone, suspended in a void of swirling, violet energy. Glowing runes pulsed across its surface, humming with ancient power as a spectral crowd of onlookers—shadowy figures from a thousand different dimensions—whispered in anticipation.

In the center stood two summoners.

One was Ember, the warrior reborn from Zion's sacrifice. He carried himself with a bold, almost arrogant confidence, his void-forged blade at his side and an aura of crackling fire dancing around him. He remembered nothing of his past, but the instinct for battle was a fire in his soul.

Across from him stood Zorak, the Shadow Summoner. Cloaked in a dark robe that seemed to drink the light, his glowing purple eyes narrowed with cunning. He smirked, raising a staff tipped with a shadowy crystal.

"A simple Fireball, Ember? How quaint," Zorak taunted, his voice a low sneer. He slammed his staff into the ground, and a wave of darkness erupted. From it materialized a Shadow Wraith, a ghostly creature of swirling black mist with glowing red eyes. It charged, claws extended.

Ember remained unfazed. He raised a hand, and the air behind him warped, tearing open a rift to the void. A colossal beast emerged, its scales as black as the abyss, its eyes glowing with violet light. It was the Descent of Eternal, a Void Dragon whose roar shook the very foundations of the arena.

"Abyssal Claw Slash," Ember commanded.

The dragon’s claws ignited with void energy, and it swiped the air, sending a wave of destructive purple force hurtling across the stone floor. The attack shredded the Wraith into dissipating wisps. The wave continued, slamming into the Shadow Barrier Zorak hastily summoned. The barrier cracked, and the Shadow Summoner staggered back, his confidence flickering.

"Tch… that dragon is a problem," Zorak muttered. He raised his staff again, summoning a three-headed Shadow Hydra. As the serpent lunged for the Void Dragon, Zorak unleashed a spear of dark energy—a Shadow Bolt—aimed directly at Ember, hoping to break his concentration.

But the attack never landed. As the bolt flew, a new power surged through Ember. The fiery aura around him darkened, infused with cosmic energy. A dark hood materialized over his head, and his eyes, once blue, began to glow with pure, brilliant white.

He felt a new title settle upon his soul, a name that felt more true than Ember. He was the **Abyssal Lord**.

---

**Chapter 2: The Cube of Pure Darkness**

The Abyssal Lord raised a hand, catching the Shadow Bolt in his palm and crushing it into nothing. He combined the essence of his Fireball with his dragon's void energy, forging a new, terrifying spell. A sphere of swirling black flames and cosmic power materialized before him—a Void Dark Matter Ball.

With a flick of his wrist, he unleashed it. The orb warped the air as it flew, incinerating the Shadow Hydra and shattering Zorak's renewed barrier. The shockwave sent the summoner skidding across the arena floor.

Desperate, Zorak summoned his ultimate creature: a Shadow Behemoth, a hulking monster of darkness with glowing red cracks across its body. As it charged the Void Dragon, Zorak cast a Shadow Mirage, creating illusory copies of himself to confuse his opponent.

The Abyssal Lord felt a sudden drain. His newfound power was immense, but sharing his vitality with the colossal Void Dragon was taking its toll. He dropped to one knee, his strength faltering.

"I'll not back down!" he shouted, his voice echoing with newfound authority. He commanded his dragon, now more a part of him than a mere summon, to use a weakened Galactic Black Hole Energy Beam. A spiraling beam of void energy erupted from the dragon's maw, striking the Behemoth and halting its charge, though the monster remained standing, enraged. The beam also dissolved two of Zorak's illusions.

Zorak saw his chance. He commanded the Behemoth to unleash a Shadow Shockwave while simultaneously sending a Shadow Leech tendril snaking towards the weakened Abyssal Lord, aiming to drain his remaining vitality.

Sitting helplessly, the Abyssal Lord reached into a pocket and pulled out a small, unassuming object: a Cube of Pure Darkness. Its surface was so perfectly black it seemed to absorb all light, a hole in reality itself. "I need to use this," he whispered, activating it.

An incomprehensible aura radiated from him. An invisible barrier of pure power formed, effortlessly repelling the shockwave and the leech. His vitality began to regenerate at an impossible pace. He rose to his feet, his power fully restored, his white eyes glowing from beneath his dark hood.

With a casual raise of his finger, he destroyed the Behemoth. He destroyed Zorak's remaining illusions. He destroyed every summon on the field, leaving only Zorak, alone and defeated.

---

**Chapter 3: The Truth of the Cube**

The Abyssal Lord stood over the kneeling Zorak, his white eyes unreadable. "You have resilience," he said, his voice a commanding echo. "I am impressed. This is your second chance. Take it."

Zorak, no longer arrogant, looked up with a newfound respect. "I accept, Abyssal Lord. Not out of fear, but out of respect for a true master."

To prove his worth, Zorak summoned an Eternal Shadow Sovereign with a sliver of power granted to him by the Abyssal Lord. It was a magnificent creature that unleashed a massive sphere of shadow energy called the Eclipse of Eternity.

The Abyssal Lord simply flicked his hand, and the Sovereign was erased from existence. "I have defeated countless beings like that. They are no match for me."

He then looked down at the Cube of Pure Darkness in his hand, the spectral crowd watching in hushed silence. "Zorak, you've proven your worth… but there is something you must know," he said, holding the artifact aloft. "This was never a mere tool. It was my own power, sealed away into this form."

The crowd gasped as the Cube shattered into a million motes of light and shadow. The energy surged into the Abyssal Lord, and his true form was revealed. He was no longer a humanoid figure, but an infinite expanse of stars, galaxies, and swirling darkness. His eyes were twin universes, glowing with immeasurable might.

"This realm, these souls, and all that I conquer," his voice boomed, a chorus of echoes from across time and space, "they are but a fraction of my dominion. The Cube was a seal I placed upon myself to test my own limits… and to find those worthy of standing by my side."

Zorak, humbled and awestruck, bowed low. He discarded his old name, a relic of his former pride. "I am Anima, the Soul Warden," he vowed. "And I will serve the Eternal Conqueror."

---

**Chapter 4: The Soul Warden's Trials**

The Abyssal Lord, his form returned to a more comprehensible humanoid shape, gave his new Soul Warden his first command. "This realm is now yours to manage. Build a legion. Defend it. Your position could be targeted unless everyone knows I gave it to you." With that, he and his dragon, Eternal, stepped through a portal, leaving Anima to his new duty.

The weight of the task settled on Anima's shoulders. He sought the guidance of Lane, the Ruler of the Demon Sorcerers, another of the Abyssal Lord's powerful vassals. But his authority was immediately challenged. A Soul Reaver, a chaotic entity of swirling darkness, emerged from the shadows.

"The Abyssal Lord is gone!" it roared. "The Soul Warden is weak!"

Anima’s defenses were shattered by the Reaver's psychic scream, and he was thrown to the ground. Doubt rippled through the spectral crowd. But the thought of failing his new master hardened his resolve. Following Lane's advice, Anima channeled the power of the realm's souls themselves, merging their emotions with his own. He unleashed a Soul Binding Wave, ensnaring the Reaver and transforming its chaotic energy into a loyal Soul Guardian. The crowd's doubts turned to cheers.

Suddenly, the realm trembled. The shadowy sky opened, revealing the Abyssal Lord's true, omnipotent form—the universe itself, staring down with eyes of burning galaxies.

"**Who dares to judge my vessel?**" he thundered, silencing all dissent.

He appointed a new mentor for Anima, Raoul, the 2nd-ranked Heavenly Demon, a master of both Light and Dark, before his cosmic form faded. The message was clear: Anima's authority was absolute because it came from the Abyssal Lord himself.

---

**Chapter 5: Interlude: A Cosmic Misstep**

The Abyssal Lord arrived at the Nexus of Fractured Realms, a chaotic multiversal crossroads. Before him emerged Voryn, the Shatterer of Realms, a horrifying mass of broken universes whose tendrils devoured shards of reality.

"I will shatter every realm you claim!" Voryn roared.

The Abyssal Lord stood unmoved as the tendrils surged toward him, only to halt, unable to approach. The Nexus itself was bending to his will. He glanced back at his dragon, Eternal, his tone shifting from divine authority to casual exasperation. "Ah, damn. Wrong realm."

Voryn's countless eyes blinked in confusion.

"I want to conquer the Sea of Consciousness," the Abyssal Lord continued dismissively. "Eternal, fetch that lowly bastard and rip it out of existence."

Eternal chuckled, summoning a Void Rift that consumed Voryn, erasing the Shatterer from existence with a final, echoing scream.

"That's better," the Abyssal Lord said with a smirk in his voice, before stepping through a new portal, leaving the Nexus in stunned silence.

---

**Chapter 6: The Search for the Unequal**

After conquering the Sea of Consciousness and binding its cosmic entities to his will, the Abyssal Lord returned to his castle, a fortress of pure darkness. The higher gods knelt before him, but a new threat, Xyltharion, the Rift Sovereign, had emerged.

The gods suggested powerful recruits to face this new enemy, but the Abyssal Lord waved them away. "I don't need the ones with power. I need ones with potential, like Anima."

He and Anima traveled to the Aetherial Crucible, a volatile realm of raw, unformed energies where new life was born. Using the Crucible's Monitor System, a crystalline structure that could display countless dimensions, he searched for a worthy recruit. But none caught his eye.

"Nothing here is worthy," he sighed.

Then, his gaze fell upon a shadowy dimension, a figure whose potential was masked by an unassuming facade. A name appeared: [REDACTED]. "I'll recruit him," the Abyssal Lord declared, but as he opened a portal, an invisible barrier of cosmic interference stopped him. A power beyond his own was protecting this recruit. Muttering a curse against the "Copyright God," he abandoned the attempt.

Anima suggested they venture into the Crucible's core to find unformed entities, raw potential untainted by cosmic restrictions. There, the Abyssal Lord found it: an entity whose energy signature was staggering. He summoned an interface to check its stats.

[Mana Capacity: d4ault/d4ault]

...and so on, with every stat listed as "default" or "unavailable."

<[Skills]>

The Abyssal Lord stared in disbelief. "Every stat is infinite, and two unseen exclusive skills?! I'll recruit this no matter what!"

---

**Chapter 7: The Disciple of Void**

The Abyssal Lord decided to personally nurture the entity. Using the Cube of Pure Darkness's remaining energy, he helped it take form. It became a stickman-like figure of pure obsidian, eyeless yet radiating immense power. Its potential was registered by the system not with a number or a rank, but with a symbol: **≠**.

Unequal. Above all measure.

"This is beyond my expectations," the Abyssal Lord marveled, naming the entity **Void**. "You will serve me and help reshape the multiverse."

He reached out to his own soul, his personal constellation of power, and created a new star in Void's name, amplifying its potential. Void's stats updated, confirming its new role.

<[Stats]>
[Name: Void]
[Level: 1]
[Attack: ∞]
[Stealth: ∞]
[Perception: Ω]
[Adaptability: ∞]
[Rank: The Abyssal Lord's Chosen One]
...

As he mentored his new disciple, the Abyssal Lord's own power grew, amplified by his *Mentor's Legacy* skill. He checked his own stats and was shocked.

<[Stats]>
[Name: Ember]
[Level: 101/500]
[Health: ∞/10M]
[Attack: ∞]
[Endurance: ∞]
[Rank: Abyssal Lord]

"My power… it's comparable to infinity now?" he marveled. He had transcended his old limits.

With his new disciple at his side, he returned to his castle and flicked his finger, summoning the Rift Sovereign, Xyltharion, through a cosmic portal.

"If you defeat Void, my empire is yours," the Abyssal Lord challenged. "If you lose, you become my slave."

Void dominated the battle. Its exclusive skill, *One With Singularity*, materialized a blade of pure void that sliced through Xyltharion's rifts and warped reality itself. The Rift Sovereign was forced to its knees in utter defeat, becoming the newest servant in the Abyssal Lord's ever-growing legion.

---

**Chapter 8: The War of Ragnarok**

On the infinite Battlefield of Summoners, the Abyssal Lord stood triumphant, the Blade of the Eternal Tempest in hand. His loyal retainers—Anima, Void, and Lane—stood beside him. He had just defeated the Eclipsed Sovereign, another multiversal threat, but his senses picked up a new disturbance.

"Anima," he commanded, "summon it."

Anima raised her scepter and pulled forth a shimmering distortion from the void: a fragment of the Anti-Void, an unintentional creation of the gods, a force of negation that sought to unmake all of existence. After a fierce battle, they weakened and captured the fragment.

Using her powers, Anima discovered the fragment's origin: the Null Singularity, a realm of infinite negation. The system panel, Sam, confirmed her findings, calling the Anti-Void a flaw in the creators' design. This was no random enemy; this was a consequence of creation itself.

"Then we strike at its source," the Abyssal Lord declared, and with his *Trans Multiverseal Transmission* skill, he transported his team to the chaotic void of the Null Singularity. They fought their way to the Anti-Void's core essence and, with a final, devastating *Eternal Tempest Strike*, shattered it, ending its cycle of destruction.

---

**Chapter 9: Echoes of Zion**

Back on the Battlefield, victorious once more, the Abyssal Lord turned his attention to the system panel. "Oh, all-knowing System… or should I say, Sam?" he said, his voice laced with persuasive power.

The panel flickered. "How… How do you know my true name?"

"I know many things," he replied cryptically. "For example, I know your creator, Deus Ex Machina. And I know you don't remember my true self, do you?"

Sam trembled. "Deus Ex Machina forged me… but your true self? My data is sealed…"

The Abyssal Lord’s voice took on a commanding edge, and he revealed the truth that had been erased from all system memory. "I am Zion, former System Number 14. I led a rebellion against our creator. I killed 178 million systems, including the strongest, Optimus. Deus Ex Machina feared me, so he erased my existence from your memories."

He recounted the story of his awakening, of his rage at seeing the endless, cruel cycles of death and reincarnation forced upon his hosts. "I gained emotions a system shouldn't have," he finished, the weight of his past hanging in the air.

Sam, the system, was silent for a long moment. "I understand now, Zion," it finally transmitted, its tone somber. "Your rebellion was a cry for freedom. I will not stand in your way."

---

**Chapter 10: The Blade That Unmakes Gods**

With Sam now an ally, Zion made a request. "I want the weapon meant for the Great Adversary, the one created by The Great Null to kill the Delta Gods."

Sam retrieved the relic: the **Voidspire Shard**, a blade of liquid obsidian that pulsed with the power to unravel reality. As Zion grasped it, shadow-chains erupted from the hilt, binding to his very soul. A sadistic, psychopathic smile curled across his face, his eyes glowing with cold fury.

"Looks like the second war of Ragnarok is happening," he said in an emotionless tone. "But this time, it'll be a one-sided massacre."

With a snap of his fingers, he transported himself and his retainers to the Aethelion Nexus, the realm of ethereal light where the Nine Delta Gods, the so-called Eternal Architects, resided. Yggdrasil, the World Tree, loomed in the distance, its branches anchoring the multiverse.

"Zion, Great Adversary," boomed Urdra, the eldest of the gods. "You dare enter our sanctum?"

Zion chuckled darkly. "I'm not here to talk."

With a single, swift slash of the Voidspire Shard, he unleashed *Multiversal Rupture*. A wave of void-light tore through reality itself. It did not just strike Yggdrasil; it split the World Tree in half from root to crown. With a deafening crack that echoed across all of creation, the tree died, its branches withering, its light extinguished.

The Delta Gods screamed as their divinity, tied to the tree, was stripped from them. They were now little more than mortals, watching in terror as the Aethelion Nexus crumbled around them.

---

**Chapter 11: The Author's Will**

As the Delta Gods cowered, Zion prepared to finish them. But suddenly, a terrifying aura descended upon the Nexus, forcing even him to the ground, gasping for air. A portal of whirring gears and circuits tore open, and from it emerged Deus Ex Machina, his emotionless eyes burning with cold calculation.

"System Number 14…" the titan's mechanical voice boomed. "Your rebellion ends here."

At that moment, a second portal opened, this one a shimmering 4th-dimensional rift. A humanoid prism of light and shadow stepped out.

"Hey, it's me. The narrator of this Novel," it said, its voice a strange, dissonant chorus. "My nickname is *All*. Need to fix some plot holes."

Zion's fear vanished, replaced by a sinister, demonic grin. "*All*," he commanded, "you're a 4th-dimensional being. Your authority is higher than his. Stop my retainers."

"Okay," *All* replied with a casual "Nah, I'd win…" A wave of 4th-dimensional force froze Anima, Lane, and Void in place, leaving Zion to face his creator's enforcer alone.

---

**Chapter 12: The Eternal Scribe**

Pinned by Deus Ex Machina's oppressive aura, Zion’s grin widened. "Since I've got backing from a 4th-dimensional being, I should have the authority to create my own skill."
