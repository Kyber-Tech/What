### **Unit 1: Rapid Multiplication Tricks**

#### **Chapter 1: The Magic of 11**
*Multiplying by 11 is the gateway to mental agility.*
*   **Two-Digit:** Separate the digits and place their sum in the middle.
    *   *Example:* $43 \times 11 \rightarrow 4 \dots (4+3) \dots 3 =$ **473**.
*   **The Carry Over:** If the sum is $\geq 10$, add the 1 to the first digit.
    *   *Example:* $68 \times 11 \rightarrow 6 \dots (14) \dots 8 \rightarrow (6+1)48 =$ **748**.
*   **Three-Digit:** Keep the ends, add the pairs.
    *   *Example:* $352 \times 11 \rightarrow 3 \dots (3+5) \dots (5+2) \dots 2 =$ **3872**.

#### **Chapter 2: Squaring Numbers Ending in 5**
*   **Method:** Multiply the first digit by the next consecutive number $(n+1)$ and append "25."
*   *Example:* $75 \times 75 \rightarrow (7 \times 8) = 56$. Result: **5625**.

#### **Chapter 3: The "Same First Digit, Sum of Ten" Rule**
*   **Condition:** First digits are identical; last digits sum to 10.
*   **Method:** $n(n+1)$ for the front; multiply the last digits for the back.
*   *Example:* $44 \times 46 \rightarrow (4 \times 5) = 20 \dots (4 \times 6) = 24$. Result: **2024**.

### **Unit 2: Vedic Arithmetic Mastery**

#### **Chapter 4: Vertical & Crosswise Multiplication**
*Solve any 2-digit multiplication ($41 \times 23$) in three visual steps:*
1.  **Left Vertical:** $4 \times 2 = 8$.
2.  **Crosswise:** $(4 \times 3) + (1 \times 2) = 14$.
3.  **Right Vertical:** $1 \times 3 = 3$.
4.  **Assembly:** $8 \dots (14) \dots 3 \rightarrow (8+1) \dots 4 \dots 3 =$ **943**.

#### **Chapter 5: Left-to-Right Subtraction (The Gap Method)**
*Forget borrowing. Find the gap between the remainders.*
*   *Example:* $313 - 117$.
    1.  Bases: $300 - 100 = 200$.
    2.  Gap: 17 is 4 higher than 13.
    3.  Adjust: $200 - 4 =$ **196**.

#### **Chapter 6: Base Approximation Division**
*Estimate complex divisions like $92 \div 41$ instantly.*
1.  **Round:** $100 \div 50 = 2$.
2.  **Adjust:** We added 9 to get to 50. Multiply that 9 by our answer (2) = 18.
3.  **Refine:** Add 18 to original numerator $(92 + 18) = 110$.
4.  **Result:** $110 \div 50 =$ **2.2**.

### **Unit 3: The 7-Stage Addition Mastery Plan**
*Follow these stages to process sums without ever using your fingers.*

*   **Stage 1:** Single Digit Instant Recall ($5+6, 9+4$).
*   **Stage 2:** Decimal Jumps ($47+7$: Knowing $7+7=14$ jumps you to 54).
*   **Stage 3:** Base Multiples ($40+50=90, 80+70=150$).
*   **Stage 4:** Double Digit (<100): $47+17 \rightarrow (47+7) + 10 = 64$.
*   **Stage 5:** Double Digit (>100): $68+74 \rightarrow (60+70) + (8+4) = 142$.
*   **Stage 6 (Compensation):** For numbers ending in 9 (e.g., $+199$). Add 200, then subtract 1.
*   **Stage 7 (Break & Connect):** Place value breakdown ($734+679 \rightarrow 1300 + 100 + 13 = 1413$).

### **Unit 4: The Psychology of the Grader**

#### **Chapter 7: Formatting for Extra Marks**
*Teachers grade based on "mental friction." The less friction, the higher the marks.*
1.  **The Keyword Strategy:** Underline technical terms with a pencil so the grader sees your knowledge immediately.
2.  **The Box Rule:** Draw a box around your final numerical answer.
3.  **The Table Hack:** Always use T-charts for comparison questions.
4.  **The Sequence Rule:** Answer in order (1, 2, 3). If you don't know one, save it for the absolute end of the paper.

### **Unit 5: Speed Writing & Info Retrieval**

#### **Chapter 8: Brain-to-Hand Coordination**
1.  **Hand Warm-up:** 30 minutes before an exam, scribble loops and fast lines for 30 seconds to wake up your motor skills.
2.  **Air Writing:** Practice writing formulas in the air to improve wrist fluidity.
3.  **The Rough Page Strategy:** Label the last page **"ROUGH."** Outline long answers there first to avoid messy "cuts" on your main sheet.
4.  **Clean Corrections:** Never scribble. Use a ruler to draw a single horizontal line through an error.
